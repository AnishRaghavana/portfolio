I developed a full social media strategy for Linjer, a sustainable fine jewelry brand competing against Mejuri with a fraction of the budget. I audited their Instagram and Facebook presence, ran a competitive analysis, mapped their audience gaps, and built out a multi-channel strategy from the ground up.

My biggest idea was #WinHerwithLinjer — a UGC campaign inviting men to share long-form stories about how they met their partner. Not a highlight reel. Not a proposal video. A thoughtful reflection on connection and vulnerability, with their Linjer wedding band as the anchor. One couple gets selected, and Linjer hosts their dream wedding. I took this concept further than the classroom — I posted it on LinkedIn, articulating the emotional insight behind it: that most jewelry campaigns speak to women, and this one deliberately invites men into the emotional landscape of marriage.

Beyond that, I designed a tiered influencer program across macro, mid, and micro creators, built a TikTok growth strategy with a full weekly content calendar, and proposed a live streaming activation to drive real-time engagement and conversions. Every recommendation tied back to a clear gap I identified in the audit — low interactivity, inconsistent storytelling, and a brand voice that looked premium but felt distant.

The throughline: Linjer's aesthetic is already there. It just needed a strategy bold enough to match it.
