I worked with Team 9 at TrustWorks to develop a $1M expansion strategy for Rodney Jones Law Group, aiming to break through the noise of the oversaturated Texas personal injury market. We steered away from the typical "aggressive" legal branding, instead focusing on a customer-first approach that prioritized community trust. I helped lead the market positioning, running deep-dive SWOT and competitive audits to find the gap where Rodney could outshine high-cost, profit-driven firms by being the more accessible and relationship-oriented choice.

To make the campaign resonate, I collaborated on the development of specific target personas, like "Head of Household Heather," ensuring our messaging spoke directly to the emotional needs of families. This research fueled our creative strategy, where I co-designed a three-part campaign—Rodney Cares, We Called Rodney, and Family First—to move the needle from simple advertisements to genuine brand advocacy. I also contributed to the art direction for TV spots that traded flashy graphics for heartfelt, testimonial-driven storytelling.

Collaborating with a team of eight taught me how to turn a massive budget and varying creative opinions into a sharp, cohesive pitch. I learned to balance high-level strategy with granular execution, from managing complex budget grids to defining the KPIs that track real growth. This project was a masterclass in how to use data-driven research to humanize a brand and turn a legal practice into a household name.
