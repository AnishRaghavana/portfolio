I built a full brand identity system for Student Design Haus — a concept campaign connecting clients with student designers who turn ideas into reality. The brand is rooted in the Bauhaus movement, drawing on its core philosophy that art and function belong together, and that students are capable of professional-grade work.

I designed the logo, developed the complete style guide, and created a promotional poster — all from scratch. The visual system leans into geometric energy: bold triangular forms, a vibrant multi-color palette pulled from Bauhaus-inspired primaries and complementary tones, and tight typographic contrast between Bauhaus 93 and Fjalla One. The result is a brand that feels energetic, modern, and creative without sacrificing professionalism.

The concept poster ties it all together with the line "Art expresses the problem, design solves it" — which is exactly what Student Design Haus does. It gives clients a real creative partner and gives students a real portfolio moment.
