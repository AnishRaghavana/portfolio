I took on a real-world brief for Mahindra USA — a tractor brand facing a David vs. Goliath problem. Up against John Deere and Kubota with over 10x the budget, I built a campaign designed to punch way above its weight class in a declining market.

I led strategy from the ground up, driving brand positioning, audience research, campaign architecture, and media planning from brief to final presentation. My biggest move was repositioning Mahindra's identity from product-first to emotionally resonant — developing the campaign theme "Family Driven. Field Tested." to capture the authenticity, grit, and rural values their buyers actually live by.

I built detailed audience personas to ground every creative and media decision in real human behavior, then designed a full multi-channel strategy spanning paid social, video, print, trade shows, a rodeo activation, and a flagship experiential concept — the Mahindra Field Fest, a country music festival built to generate 15–20K qualified impressions in a single day. I also structured a lean influencer partnership timed to National Agriculture Day, trading product for authentic content instead of burning budget.

My strategy was built to deliver: +15% dealership visits, +30% social engagement, +20% website sessions, and +1% market share growth by Q2 — all tracked against hard KPIs. The through-line across everything was a simple insight: rural buyers don't just buy tractors, they buy identity. I built a campaign that spoke that language fluently.
