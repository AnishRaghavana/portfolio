Artist Boat — Campaign Executions

01
Houston Astros Field Day & First Pitch
Artist Boat throws the first pitch at an Astros home game — then takes the field. An interactive Field Day at Daikin Park with two Astros players, Artist Boat staff, and volunteers. $30 entry includes a campaign shirt. 33,000 fans per home game. One of the most culturally connected activation moments in Houston sports.
Est. 100,000 impressions  ·  $50 CPM
02
Galveston Drone Light Show
A nighttime drone show over Galveston Beach in November — timed near Thanksgiving as an alternative to fireworks. Drones display wildlife, landscapes, and campaign graphics over the ocean. Art meets science in the sky. A spectacle that connects the audience physically to the coast they’re being asked to save.
Est. 250,000 impressions  ·  $40 CPM  ·  Zero pollution
03
Shoreline Stride — The 5K
A 5K–10K marathon routed through the preserved land Artist Boat is actively saving — so runners experience the mission on foot. 66.8% of respondents said they’d volunteer for a charity. This activation turns that willingness into action, community, and a campaign moment that generates organic content and long-term donor relationships.
19% of surveyed audience enjoys outdoor activities  ·  Custom shirts + race medals

Anish Raghavan — Account Director

Led a 20-person agency team across Account, Media, and Creative to develop a $100K integrated campaign for Artist Boat, a nonprofit coastal conservation organization. Responsible for shaping campaign strategy, maintaining cross-departmental alignment, and assembling the final plansbook. Built on 660 survey responses, three audience personas, and a $16M fundraising goal. The campaign insight — connecting Houston’s "Be Someone" graffiti to coastal stewardship — was the strategic and creative foundation for everything that followed.
